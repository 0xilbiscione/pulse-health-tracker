FitBase — User guide

A few numbers a day, turned into momentum · track, set goals, see your trends

Welcome to FitBase. Log a few numbers each day — steps, weight, meals, sleep — and FitBase turns them into a warm dashboard with goal rings, streaks, and weekly insights. Everything below takes about five minutes to learn.

1. Getting started

  1. Open fit.metricbase.org in any browser.
  2. Click Create one on the sign-in screen and enter your name, email, and a password (at least 6 characters).
  3. You'll land on your Dashboard. It starts mostly empty — that's expected until you log your first entry.
Just want a look around? Sign in with the demo account — email demo@health.app, password demo1234 — to see a dashboard pre-filled with 30 days of sample data.

Your data is private to your account. Other users can never see your entries.

2. Logging an entry

FitBase keeps one entry per day. Go to Log entry in the menu, pick the date if needed, and fill in whatever you have — every field is optional. The form is split into four tabs:

Activity

  • Steps, distance (km)
  • Active minutes
  • Workout type & minutes

Body

  • Weight (kg), body fat %
  • Blood pressure (systolic / diastolic)
  • Resting heart rate

Nutrition

  • Calories
  • Protein / carbs / fat (g)
  • Water (ml)

Sleep & Mood

  • Sleep hours & quality (1–5)
  • Mood, energy, stress (1–5)
  • Free-text notes
  1. Fill any fields across the four tabs.
  2. Press Save entry. A green “Saved” confirmation appears.
  3. Re-open the same day to edit in place, or use Delete to remove that day.

Tip: log at the same time each day (e.g. before bed) to keep your streak alive.

How repeated saves work

You can log the same day as many times as you like — there's always just one entry per day, and it updates each time you save.

  • Activity & Nutrition values add up. Steps, distance, active/workout minutes, calories, protein, carbs, fat, and water are added to the day's running total. Log 3,000 steps in the morning and 5,000 later and the day shows 8,000. These fields start empty and show a “Today so far” hint.
  • Body & Sleep/Mood values replace. Weight, body fat, blood pressure, resting heart rate, sleep, and the 1–5 ratings keep your latest reading — they don't add up.

Made a typo in a running total? On the Activity or Nutrition tab, switch the toggle from Add to Set total. The fields pre-fill with the current totals so you can edit them directly and save.

3. Setting goals

Open Goals to choose daily targets. Toggle a goal on, type a target, and press Save. Active goals appear as progress rings on your dashboard.

GoalTypeMeaning
Steps, Active minutes, Water, SleepReach or exceedRing fills as you approach the target; full when you hit it.
Calories, WeightStay at or underRing is full while you're at or below target.

New accounts start with sensible defaults (e.g. 10,000 steps, 2,000 ml water, 8 hours sleep). Change them any time.

4. Reading your dashboard

  • Stat cards — today's key numbers with a small arrow showing this week vs last week (green = a good change, red = worth a look).
  • Goal rings — today's progress toward each active goal.
  • Logging streak — consecutive days you've logged anything. A flame marks your best current goal streak too.
  • Trend charts — steps, sleep, water, and weight over the last 30 days.
  • This week's insights — short auto-generated notes about what's improving or slipping.

5. Insights page

  • Weekly averages — every metric, this week vs last, with a trend arrow.
  • Quick totals — days logged, weekly step total, water in litres, average sleep.
  • Daily bars — steps, water, active minutes, and calories per day; bars below your goal are faded so gaps stand out.

6. Tips & FAQ

How often should I log?

Once a day is ideal. Consistency matters more than filling every field — even just steps and sleep produces useful trends.

Can I log a past day?

Yes. On Log entry, use the date picker to select any earlier date, then save.

I left fields blank — is that OK?

Absolutely. Blank fields are simply ignored; charts connect across days that have data.

I logged the wrong number — how do I fix it?

For an activity or nutrition total, switch the Add / Set total toggle to Set total, correct the value, and save. For body and sleep/mood fields just retype the value and save — those replace the previous reading.

Which units does FitBase use?

Metric: kilograms, kilometres, millilitres, and kilocalories. Quality, mood, energy, and stress use a simple 1–5 scale.

How do I sign out?

Use the sign-out icon in the top-right of the header.

Is my data shared?

No. Entries are tied to your account and visible only to you.
